# D&D Session Summary - Infiltrating the Bandit Camp & Boss Fight

## Session Overview
The party located the Forsaken bandit camp on an island in a lake, infiltrated it through a combination of disguise and bluffing, negotiated with a bandit sergeant to split their forces, and then fought and defeated the bandit leader Alvida in her fortified headquarters.

---

## Getting to the Island

The bandit camp was built on an island roughly a football field away across a lake. The party spotted a raft on the shore that turned out to be a trap - it had been soaked in flammable liquid and was poorly constructed to fall apart under impact. Searching the tree line, they found a second hidden raft that was safe to use.

The party debated how to approach: swimming, using the decoy raft as a distraction, or sneaking around the shore. They ultimately decided the best play was disguise - two members would pose as bandits escorting prisoners.

---

## The Infiltration

### Getting In
While crossing, the party noticed the lake had submerged wooden spikes as a defensive measure, but were able to navigate around them. On the island, they spotted a guard sleeping off a bottle near the gate. The party tied him up, took his clothes, and used them to bluff their way through the front gate.

The acting worked well enough to get them inside. The camp interior was an old abandoned fort the Forsaken had taken over. A dirt courtyard with training dummies and target posts sat in the center, surrounded by canvas tents. The party estimated around 32 bandits total in the camp.

### Meeting Thurston
A bandit sergeant named Thurston led them to a stone room where five bandits were playing cards. When questioned about the "prisoners," the party's cover started to slip - only one of them was wearing actual bandit clothing, which drew suspicion. Vivian improvised a story about being a new recruit named Bob who had caught the others trying to cross the river.

Thurston bought it enough to let them stay, though he mentioned they'd need to meet Alvida before things were settled. The party took this as an opportunity.

---

## Negotiating with Thurston

Rather than immediately fighting, the party decided to try talking Thurston around. They made the case that Alvida's plan to take over the village and crown herself queen wasn't profitable - there was no gold in a backwater farming town. Why stay loyal to someone chasing that when there were better targets?

Thurston turned out to be motivated by freedom and money, both of which Alvida's "queen" ambitions threatened. The party appealed directly to those motivations. The negotiation succeeded: Thurston agreed to take his people out on a raid against the Gilded Hand mercenaries - a more profitable target - while the party dealt with Alvida. This effectively split the bandit camp, leaving the party to face Alvida with far fewer enemies around.

**Victory Point earned** for successfully navigating the infiltration and negotiation.

---

## Boss Fight - Alvida

### The Setup
The party entered Alvida's room under the pretense of being sent by Thurston. She had one companion - a robed woman named Lacey. The party used the element of surprise to open combat before Alvida could react.

Wilhelm (the retainer) joined the fight alongside the party.

### The Fight
Alvida proved to be a dangerous opponent. She wielded a magic sword and whip combination, hitting multiple party members for heavy damage with area attacks. Her companion Lacey threw flasks of corruption damage and used a psionic knife that caused additional corruption effects on top of her strikes.

Key moments in the fight:
- **Alvida's opening was brutal** - she hit multiple party members in quick succession, knocking Vivian and others close to dying
- **Lacey targeted the party's Healer** specifically, trying to take Aaron out of the fight with targeted corruption attacks
- **Wilhelm went down** mid-fight from taking sustained damage - the party made the difficult call not to spend resources reviving him since he added little damage output and heals were needed elsewhere
- **Aaron's prayers kept the party standing**, spending piety throughout to heal Vivian and Bree back from dying and removing conditions
- **Bree dealt the bulk of the damage**, using surges and signature attacks to chunk Alvida's health down
- **Bo's mark** gave the party consistent edge on attacks throughout the fight
- **Vivian turned the tide late** with a drain ability that dealt corruption damage and triggered a recovery chain that stabilized the party

Alvida was eventually finished off, with the killing blow landing when the party coordinated a round of attacks that overwhelmed her remaining health.
