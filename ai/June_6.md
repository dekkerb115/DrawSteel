# D&D Session Summary - Post-Battle Recovery & Village Investigation

## Session Overview
Following the werewolf boss battle, the party focused on roleplay and investigation. They administered the lycanthropy cure to both infected NPCs, accidentally created a potential were-pig, and uncovered that a starving woodcutter's son had been stealing grain to feed his family. New threats emerged in the form of a bandit group called The Forsaken, possibly connected to a historical mercenary company known as the Jagged Edge.

## The Accidental Were-Pig

During the respite, Big Jeb was discovered happily eating the werewolf carcass. The pig had consumed enough remains to potentially contract lycanthropy. After a lively debate, the party decided to keep him and embrace the possibility of a were-pig companion. A secure enclosure will need to be built before the next full moon. A vial of werewolf blood was also set aside for potential future experiments.

## Village Interactions

### Mikael the Carpenter
- Successfully treated with the lycanthropy cure
- Warned that the woods may still have water elementals present but that the werewolf threat is gone
- Encouraged to resume work when safe to do so

### Vaughn the Woodcutter
- Found visibly exhausted and hungry, patrolling his property with a young companion named Percy
- Had been unable to cut wood for nearly a month due to a bandit group calling themselves **The Forsaken** blocking his usual area
- The family was slowly running out of food
- Committed to cutting younger trees closer to town in the meantime to make some income
- As a thank you, gave the party **3 Snapdragon flowers** - rare magical blooms that grant a temporary boost to strength when sniffed

### The Reeve
- Village leader, notable for old claw mark scars down her face and an eye patch
- Confirmed she'd heard about the bandits from Vaughn but had no one qualified to investigate
- Granted the party permission to look into a series of grain thefts from the community granary
- Shared historical background on **The Jagged Edge** - a mercenary company hired roughly 40 years ago by someone calling herself a queen to seize control of the region. An uprising with imperial support stopped them, but most of the village was destroyed in the process. The name may have been revived by a new group.
- The Forsaken bandits Vaughn encountered may be connected to this history

### Robin and Sarah (The Mill)
- Owners of the granary and mill on the river
- Robin reported seeing a shadowy figure crawling out from under the grain store at night, sack in hand, fleeing toward the woods in the direction of Vaughn's home
- Investigation of the granary revealed a hidden trapdoor cut with a small saw - the kind of tool only a carpenter or woodcutter would own

### Percy's Confession
- When confronted separately, Vaughn's young companion Percy broke down and admitted he had been the one stealing the grain
- With Vaughn unable to work for a month due to the bandits, the family had been quietly starving
- Percy had been sneaking out at night to steal just enough to get by
- The party chose not to report him to the Reeve
- Plan: deal with the bandits first so Vaughn can get back to work, which solves the root problem without punishing a desperate kid

A bandit group calling themselves The Forsaken has been operating in the forest, blocking Vaughn's access to his cutting area and openly talking about robbing and murdering townspeople. They may be connected to the historical Jagged Edge mercenary company. The party plans to investigate their camp next session.
