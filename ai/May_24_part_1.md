# D&D Session Summary - Cave Battle with Water Elementals

## Session Overview
The party engaged in combat with water elementals and their alpha in the cave while attempting to rescue the shepherd's brother. The fight took place around a rushing underground river with challenging terrain.

---

## Session Start - Recap

### Previous Events Summary
- Party identified the werewolf threat and learned Mikael the carpenter was bitten
- Mikael contracted lycanthropy and was warned about the next full moon
- Party tracked the werewolf to a cave where they found a frightened shepherd
- The shepherd's brother was dragged into the cave by a large wolf
- Shepherd offered wool from his sheep in exchange for rescue
- Party entered the cave and discovered water elementals in an underground river

### Combat Setup
- Cave chamber with a large, glowing underground river running through it
- River is approximately 25 feet deep (5 squares)
- Rocks jutting from the river create potential stepping stones but are treacherous
- Party attempted to communicate with water elementals but failed
- Combat initiated when party was surprised by the elementals

## Enemy Forces

### Water Elementals Composition
- **1 Alpha Water Elemental** (Large, marked as "big daddy" with crown token)
- **Multiple Water Elemental Minions** organized in groups:
  - Group 1 & 2 (minions)
  - Group 3 (minions) 
  - Group 4 (minions)

### Enemy Abilities Discovered
- **Submerge and Emerge**: Can sink into water and emerge from any connected body of water (teleportation through water)
- **Rally**: Alpha used a 5-burst maneuver giving all allies within range an edge on attacks until its next turn
- **Bite and Throw**: Bite attack that can fling targets into the water
- **Drag**: Pull ability that forces targets toward the water (3 squares of forced movement)
- **Fire Resistance**: Water elementals take reduced damage from fire attacks
- **Immunity**: Some level of damage immunity/reduction

## Key Mechanics Clarified

### Triggered Actions
- Can be used once per round
- Examples: Quickness Ward (shift after taking damage), Careful Stitches (damage reflection)
- Party members have various triggered defensive abilities

### Forced Movement & Stability
- Base forced movement: 3 squares
- Stability feature: Reduces forced movement by 1 (to 2 squares)
- Shift movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks

### Water Combat Rules
- Swimming costs 2 movement squares per square traveled (without swim speed)
- Actions taken while in water suffer penalties
- Characters can tread water but take banes on actions

### Hunter's Mark Effects
- Marked target: Allies gain edge on power rolls against it
- Can spend resources for additional effects (extra damage equal to 2x Reason score)
- Can be moved to new target as bonus action

### Bonded Ring Effects
- +6 maximum stamina
- Shared recovery pool (use highest recovery value)
- Damage sharing: All bonded creatures take 1 damage when any takes damage
