# D&D Session Summary - Cave Battle with Water Elementals

## Session Overview
Picking up where the party left off, the group entered the cave and encountered water elementals in a large underground river chamber. After failing to communicate with them, combat broke out. The fight was challenging due to the difficult river terrain and the elementals' ability to teleport through water.

---

## Setting the Scene

The cave opened into a large chamber with a fast-moving, glowing underground river cutting through the center. Stepping stones jutted from the water but were treacherous. The party had attempted to talk their way past the elementals on the way in but failed, triggering a surprise round in the elementals' favor.

---

## The Water Elemental Fight

### Enemy Forces
The party faced a large alpha water elemental and several groups of minions. The elementals proved difficult to deal with due to their ability to submerge and re-emerge anywhere along the river, effectively teleporting around the battlefield. Fire attacks were noticeably less effective against them due to their water nature.

Key abilities the party discovered:
- **Submerge and Emerge** - could sink into the river and pop up anywhere along it
- **Bite and Throw** - could fling party members into the water
- **Drag** - forced party members toward the water's edge
- **Rally** - the alpha buffed all nearby allies at the start of combat

### How the Fight Unfolded
The elementals struck first, with the alpha landing a critical hit on Bo and flinging him into the river. Several minion groups teleported into flanking positions around the party. Vivian retaliated with a fire area attack that killed two minions outright, though the damage was reduced by the elementals' fire resistance. Bo climbed out of the water onto a rock and marked the alpha, giving the whole party an advantage against it. Bree moved to safer ground and picked off another minion. Aaron healed himself and set up his damage reflection ability to punish enemies that hit his allies.

By the end of the first round the alpha was bloodied, several minions were dead, and the party had established better positioning - but the fight was far from over.

### Party Challenges
- The river made movement costly and dangerous
- Anyone pulled into the deep water was at risk of drowning
- The bonded rings meant every hit on one character also dealt chip damage to the other wearer
- Fire, the party's main damage type, was partially resisted

---

## Items & Mechanics Confirmed

### Bonded Rings (Bo and Aaron both wearing)
- Grant +6 maximum stamina
- Allow the wearer to use the highest recovery value in the bonded group
- Downside: when one bonded creature takes damage, all others take 1 damage that cannot be reduced

### Wool Acquired
- The shepherd outside the cave promised wool from his sheep in exchange for rescuing his brother
- Wool is in the party's possession for crafting Jeb's sweater
